Round Robin Golf Format: Rules, Pairings and Scoring

Round Robin is a golf format where pairings rotate so everyone partners with everyone and plays against everyone over the round. In a four-player group, the standard setup uses three six-hole segments with a different 2v2 team each segment. The format is popular for member-guests, league days, and trip rounds because it spreads variance and keeps every segment meaningful.

If your group wants less teammate luck and more full-group fairness, Round Robin is the cleanest format. Every six holes, teams reshuffle. Nobody is stuck with one partner all day, and every player has multiple chances to win points across changing matchups.

What Is Round Robin?

In golf, Round Robin usually means fixed rotations of two-player teams across an 18-hole round. The goal is balance: each player gets equal partner exposure and equal opponent exposure. That makes outcomes less dependent on one hot or cold teammate.

Most groups play Round Robin as three mini-matches of six holes each. Each mini-match has its own result and points, then all results are totaled at the end. Because every segment starts fresh, bad early holes do not bury the day.

Round Robin is the format for groups that want fair rotations, tight action, and fewer arguments about who got the easy side of the draw.

How to Play Round Robin

  1. Confirm four players. Round Robin is most elegant with exactly four players. You can scale up to larger fields, but four-player pods are the standard unit.
  2. Split into three six-hole segments. Use holes 1-6, 7-12, and 13-18 so each segment has equal weight.
  3. Set your scoring model. Common options are match-play points, holes won, or net score totals with handicap adjustment.
  4. Rotate teams each segment. Use the standard partner rotation shown below so everyone gets each partner once.
  5. Settle as net totals. Convert points to dollars and settle final balances after hole 18.

This gives each player exactly one segment with each other player as partner. It also ensures everyone faces every opponent pairing across the day.

Scoring and Settlement

A practical scoring setup is 2 points for winning a segment, 1 for halving, 0 for losing. You can also pay per hole won if your group prefers more volatility.

Strategy Tips

Winning More Round Robin Segments
  • Start fast in segment one. Early points reduce pressure later and force opponents to chase.
  • Adapt to partner profile. With a steady partner, play aggressive lines; with a volatile partner, protect doubles.
  • Treat each segment as a reset. Do not carry tilt from prior holes into the next rotation.
  • Use handicap strokes proactively. Plan around stroke holes before each segment begins.
  • Play for halves when out of position. A halved mini-match preserves equity and limits downside.

Common Disputes

Do We Reset Scores Between Segments?

For match-play style Round Robin, yes. Each six-hole segment is its own match and starts all square.

How Do Handicaps Work in Round Robin?

Apply strokes by the index ranking of holes played in each segment. Keep the method consistent across all segments.

Can We Use Nassau Instead of Segment Points?

Yes. Many groups run each six-hole segment as its own Nassau mini-game. Just define presses and carryovers in advance.
