Product & Positioning

What Waggle is, who it's for, and why it wins.

Core Positioning

"The sportsbook for your golf event."

One link. Every phone. Live odds, real-time scores, and automatic settlement — no app download required. Built for boys trips and member-guest tournaments.

What Waggle Does

Waggle turns any golf event into a fully featured sportsbook. The organizer creates an event in under five minutes, sets the format and games (Nassau, skins, wolf, vegas, banker, bloodsome, stableford), and shares one link with the group. Every player bets from their phone — no account creation, no app. Scores post in real time as the round progresses. When the last putt drops, everything settles automatically and credits post instantly.

Competitive Landscape

Competitor What They Do Price Waggle Advantage
Group Text / Spreadsheet Manual tracking, someone's always doing math wrong Free Automated, live, no disputes. Frees the organizer.
Golf Genius Club tournament management, scoring, handicaps $500+/yr Built-in betting layer, live odds, no per-seat licensing

GolfGameBook / 18Birdies Scoring apps with social features Free / $5–8/mo Real sportsbook layer with live money lines, not just leaderboards

Primary Differentiators

  • No app download — runs in the browser from a shared link
  • Live odds on every match, not just static scoring
  • Betting from every phone simultaneously
  • Automatic settlement — no post-round math or disputes
  • Built for groups, not individual golfers
  • Event-based pricing (not per-seat or subscription for casual users)

Category We're Creating

Waggle isn't a scoring app with tipping. It's a sportsbook engine purpose-built for the social golf event — a category that doesn't have a clear market leader yet. The closest analog is DraftKings for NFL office pools, but there's no equivalent for the 12-person boys trip or the 40-couple member-guest.

The total addressable market: ~25 million recreational golfers in the US who participate in organized group events annually.

Mike is 38, works in finance or sales, plays to an 8 handicap. He's been organizing the annual Myrtle Beach trip with his college buddies for six years. He's good at it — books the rental house, sets up the matches, runs the Nassau. But every year it ends with someone's spreadsheet being wrong and a minor argument about who owes what. He's tech-savvy, has a Venmo, and would happily pay $29 if it meant the math happened automatically and he looked like a hero to the group.

Persona: Susan, Tournament Director

Susan is the head of the golf committee at a 400-member private club in Connecticut. She volunteers 5 hours a week running events. Golf Genius works fine for scoring but costs the club $600/year and she's the only one who knows how to use it. She'd pay $149 for a product that handles scoring, betting, and settlement in one place, especially if she can demo it first.

The words that convert browsers into buyers.

Core Message

"Turn your golf event into a real sportsbook. In five minutes."

The secondary message bridges product and emotion: "No more chasing Venmo. No more spreadsheet disputes. Everyone bets from their phone, scores update live, and everything settles automatically when the last putt drops."

T
Trip Organizer: "Your group chat doesn't need another spreadsheet. It needs a sportsbook. $29 for the whole trip — less than a sleeve of Pro V1s."
C
Club Director: "Your members expect more than a leaderboard. Give them live odds, match betting, and a settlement screen that works itself out. From $149 per event."
P
Club Pro: "Recommend Waggle to your members and get paid $20 every time someone runs an event. Set it up once — earn on every tournament they use it for."

Objection Handling

Objection "Isn't this too complicated for a casual trip?"
Response The organizer sets it up — everyone else just taps a link. No accounts, no downloads. If you can share a Google Doc you can run Waggle. We have a demo you can walk through in 90 seconds.
Objection "$29 seems steep for one weekend."
Response Split it 8 ways and it's $3.63/person — less than a round of beers at the turn. And you never have to be the person who gets the math wrong again.
Objection "We already use Golf Genius."
Response Golf Genius is great for scoring. It doesn't have a betting layer — live odds, match wagering, side bets, auto-settlement. Waggle is what you add on top of scoring, or replace it with entirely for $349 less per year.
Objection "Is this legal?"
Response Waggle facilitates social betting between players — the same as your group's existing Nassau or skins game. We're not a licensed sportsbook; we're a scorecard that handles the math automatically.
Objection "What if someone doesn't have a smartphone?"
Response Someone can always enter scores on behalf of a player, just like a paper scorecard. The rest of the group still benefits from live odds and automatic settlement.

Affiliate Club Program

Turn club pros into a scalable distribution channel.

Year 1 Target

50 active club pro affiliates × 3 events each = 150 events = ~$22,000 ARR from a single channel with zero ongoing cost per event.

At $149/event average and $20 referral payout, net margin on affiliate-sourced events is ~87% — higher than direct because there's no paid acquisition cost.

Referral Payout

$20

Per event created through a pro's affiliate link. Paid monthly via Stripe or Venmo. No cap. Stacks on every event the referred club runs — even if the organizer comes back directly next time.

Year 1 ARR Target

~$22k

Based on 50 active pros each referring an average of 3 events per year at a blended $149 average event price. Conservative — many club pros have 10+ qualifying events annually.

Active Pro Target

50

Club professionals activated in Year 1. Requires outreach to approximately 500 pros (10% activation rate). Sourced from NGCOA directories, PGA professional databases, and manual research.

Events Per Activated Pro

3+

Conservative estimate of events per affiliated pro per year. Typical club with an active pro runs 8–15 organized events annually. Even at 20% attachment rate, that's 2–3 events per pro.

Program Mechanics

What Club Pros Get

  • Unique referral link (e.g., betwaggle.com/pebblebeach)
  • $20 per event created through their link
  • Optional "Powered by [Club Name]" branding on event pages
  • Monthly payout statement
  • Co-branded email templates they can send to members
  • Priority support for their events
  • Ability to offer first event free to new club members

Outreach & Onboarding Flow

  • Week 1: Build list of 500 club pros from NGCOA + PGA directories
  • Week 2: First email (personal, short — not a newsletter)
  • Week 3: Follow-up with demo link + affiliate landing page
  • Week 4: Activated pros get onboarding call (15 min) + link setup
  • Ongoing: Monthly "pro report" email showing their events + earnings
  • Goal: 50 activated by Day 90
Branding Consideration

The "Powered by [Club Name]" option is a Trojan horse for word-of-mouth. When a member-guest player sees "Waggle — Powered by Winged Foot" on their event page, they associate the quality of the experience with both the club and Waggle. They go home and tell their own club's pro about it. The club pro becomes a free salesperson without even trying.

Why $29 / $149?

The $29 price point clears a buyer's mental "is this worth it" bar instantly — it's less than a sleeve of Pro V1s or the first round of drinks at the 19th hole. At $3.63/person for 8 players, the organizer can easily split the cost or absorb it as the "guy who runs the trip." The $149 club price is anchored against Golf Genius ($500+/year), making it feel cheap for a single event. It also clears $100, which is the psychological threshold for "this must be worth something."

Upgrade Path

Trip organizers who run Waggle 3+ times per year (roughly 15% of buyers) are the natural path to the annual subscription. The $499/yr plan is targeted at clubs running 5+ events — at $149/event, they break even at 3.3 events. The pitch for the subscription is simple: "If you run the member-guest, the club championship, and a member-member, you've already paid for it." Annual subscribers also become evangelists — "Powered by [Club Name]" branding drives organic discovery.
